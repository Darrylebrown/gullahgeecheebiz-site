## Commercial 1 — The Full House (~48s)

### Voiceover

Where rivers remember... and roots run deep.

Welcome to Gullah Geechee Biz — a cultural media house documenting Gullah Geechee people, places, and traditions so the record can never fade.

Explore Roots and Rivers encyclopedias and books. Listen on podcast and audiobook. Stream Sea Island music. Follow the story on TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Pinterest. Shop heritage merch. Visit the archive online.

Subscribe to Gullah Geechee Biz. Preserve the past. Inspire the future.

### Shot list (activities highlighted)

1. Aerial sunrise over Lowcountry marsh / river — brand open, cultural landscape
2. Stack of navy/gold encyclopedia books — Roots & Rivers + publishing house
3. Podcast / audiobook studio (mic, headphones, open book) — audio layer
4. Phone + tablet + content cards desk — TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Pinterest
5. Sweetgrass basket + heritage tee + tote on porch — merch + craft culture
6. Book on dock at marsh sunset — end card CTA

### Activities covered

- Cultural documentation mission  
- Roots & Rivers encyclopedias / books (KDP)  
- Podcast + audiobook (Spotify / Audible / ACX)  
- Sea Island music  
- Social distribution (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Pinterest)  
- Heritage merch (Printful / Stripe)  
- Online archive / site  
- Soft subscribe CTA @GullahGeecheeBiz

## Commercial 2 — The Living Record (~48s)

### Voiceover

Where rivers remember... and roots run deep.

From Beaufort to Savannah and across the corridor, Gullah Geechee Biz builds the full cultural stack: encyclopedia volumes, Kindle and Audible titles, podcast episodes, music releases, daily social storytelling, Pinterest discovery, and merch that funds the work.

This is more than content. It is a living record of America's Sea Island culture.

Subscribe to Gullah Geechee Biz. Preserve the past. Inspire the future.

### Shot list (activities highlighted)

1. Hands weaving sweetgrass basket — living craft / cultural practice  
2. Historic corridor street + Spanish moss — place / heritage corridor  
3. Open encyclopedia with marsh map — Roots & Rivers authority layer  
4. Vinyl, headphones, drum, shell shaker, mic — music + podcast  
5. Phone-on-tripod filming merch + pin cards — social + merch pipeline
